Phase I - Read and frame

Days 1-30

Build the operating picture

  • Listen with CPO, VP Product Management, product engineering, UX, growth, sales, program, support, contracts/acquisition, and mission leaders.
  • Review product strategies, roadmaps, backlogs, discovery evidence, release decisions, support trends, deployment patterns, and competitive positioning.
  • Map user-access constraints across open, restricted, and classified environments.
  • Identify where top-down direction conflicts with bottom-up product evidence.
  • Establish a coaching baseline with PMs and UX designers through working sessions, not evaluation theater.
  • Inventory current AI-assisted product and engineering practices and where human review remains essential.

Outputs

OutputPurpose
Portfolio decision mapWho decides investment, release, integration, evidence, and escalation
Mission/user access mapHow teams discover and validate in constrained environments
Support and integration signal inventoryRecurring work that may justify productization
PM/UX craft baselineWhere coaching and operating friction have highest leverage
Days 31-60

Install a minimal shared contract

  • Co-create the Mission Window Contract using existing language where possible.
  • Select three portfolio hypotheses tied to live product or market decisions.
  • Define qualitative scorecard fields and baseline collection.
  • Clarify portfolio vs. product-team decision rights.
  • Pilot AI-assisted discovery synthesis, backlog hygiene, product requirement critique, test generation, and decision-record drafting with explicit review boundaries.

Phase II - Prove and institutionalize

Days 61-90

Run live Mission Window Reviews

  • Use one agentic-orchestration, one information-operations, and one sensor/CBRNE decision as the first review set.
  • Make mission outcome, authority, integration burden, evidence, acquisition, and learning return explicit.
  • Resolve at least one capacity conflict and one platform-vs-product boundary with named owners and evidence.
  • Connect roadmap language with growth positioning, support burden, and engineering dependencies.
  • Coach PM and UX leads through the decision work; document what is reusable and what must remain local.
Days 91-120

Make learning compound

  • Institutionalize a quarterly portfolio review and a lighter monthly evidence cadence.
  • Return field, support, deployment, and evaluation evidence into product and platform roadmaps.
  • Publish a concise executive roadmap narrative: bets, evidence, dependencies, acquisition path, and stop conditions.
  • Establish the next two coaching priorities for PM/UX and the next two AI-assisted tradecraft experiments.
  • Retire, merge, or simplify artifacts that do not improve actual decisions.

Targets should be set after baselines are established. This plan deliberately avoids fabricated percentages or assumptions about undisclosed internal performance.
