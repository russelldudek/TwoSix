# Two Six Technologies Brand Fidelity Audit

Audit date: 2026-07-21

- Visible company identity: source passed. The official Two Six Technologies mark appears above the fold.
- Official logo/wordmark: used from an official company-hosted asset and stored locally.
- Independent-candidate distinction: passed. The lockup and footer identify the experience as candidate work and include a non-endorsement statement.
- Color token provenance: passed. Black, white, paper, ink, muted, and rule values are documented as official or source-sampled; Mission Window accents are labeled candidate-original.
- Typography decision: passed. The implementation documents the observed public typographic character, uses licensed substitutes, and stores no proprietary font files.
- Committed brand assets: passed at source level. Required identity assets are local.
- Document brand continuity: passed at source level. The website and application documents share the same logo, black-and-white hierarchy, rules, and Mission Window grammar.
- Product/interface cues: passed. The campaign translates high-contrast technical surfaces and precise rectilinear geometry into original candidate graphics rather than copying a proprietary interface.
- Endorsement risk: passed. The campaign does not present itself as an official Two Six publication.

Rendered logo geometry, responsive identity, and PDF fidelity are enforced by the browser and generated-document workflows.
