## Typography evidence and decision

The current public site uses a contemporary, high-contrast grotesk/geometric sans character: tightly set headlines, restrained uppercase labels, compact technical navigation, and clear weight contrast. The exact proprietary production face is not claimed or copied.

Implementation uses Inter with Noto Sans fallback because both are licensed and broadly available. The campaign recreates the observed character through weight, spacing, case, line height, and density rather than claiming the substitute is official. No proprietary font files are stored in the repository.

## Photography, product, and interface decision

No official photography or proprietary product-interface capture is reproduced. The campaign uses the official logo plus original role-derived graphics so it does not imply access to internal systems or ownership of pictured deployments.

Public product cues translated into the campaign:

- high-contrast dark technical surfaces;
- thin rules and rectilinear interface geometry;
- compact uppercase system labels;
- selective luminous signals rather than decorative gradients;
- precise, bounded motion that resolves to a stable decision state.

## Brand-to-role translation

The Mission Window Synchronizer converts portfolio leadership into a visual decision system. Product scenarios change mission pull, human authority, integration fit, acquisition path, evidence posture, and resulting action. The artifact is original candidate work and does not recreate a Two Six product interface.

## Motion character

- Fast enough to communicate operational responsiveness.
- One-shot rather than continuously distracting.
- Causal: product selection updates every dependent state together.
- Stable: each transition resolves to a readable end state.
- Accessible: keyboard, touch, and reduced-motion equivalents remain complete.

## Use by artifact

- Candidate vision: full black-and-white identity system, official logo, role-derived luminous states, and interactive decision model.
- Resume and cover letter: restrained logo, black/white structure, compact rules, and minimal signal color.
- Interview brief and entry plan: dark covers, precise page furniture, Mission Window terminology, and consistent decision tables.
- Mission Window Review: role-specific operating artifact with the same contract fields and disposition language.
- PDFs: generated from the corresponding HTML source so brand and content do not drift.

## Overall character

Technical, exact, mission-aware, contemporary, and decisive without impersonating an official corporate publication.
