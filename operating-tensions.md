# Operating Tensions

1. **Frontier technology versus mission assurance** — agentic speed must preserve traceability, evaluation, fallback, and human authority.
2. **Portfolio leverage versus product specificity** — shared platform economics must not flatten distinct operator workflows or mission consequences.
3. **Empowered teams versus portfolio direction** — teams need local judgment while executives need explicit investment, sequencing, and stop decisions.
4. **Product economics versus support reality** — deployment, integration, training, and sustainment work must become visible learning rather than hidden labor.
5. **Top-down strategy versus bottom-up evidence** — market direction and leadership intent must be tested against user, field, support, and product evidence.

The candidate thesis manages these tensions rather than pretending they disappear.
